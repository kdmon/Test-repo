Welcome to the EPIC-BT trial

This is the webpage for the ongoing UK online clinical trial entitled: Enhancing Prescription of Irradiated and CMV-negative Blood Transfusions (EPIC-BT).

To learn more, tap or click to expand a section below.

Objectives

The aim of the trial is to provide answers to the following questions:

  1. What is the current state of knowledge of irradiated and CMV-negative blood component use among prescribers of transfusion?

  2. How does the use of a local guideline or an app affect the prescription of irradiated and CMV-negative blood transfusions?

  3. How do prescribers rate existing guidelines and interventions?

Background

Although blood transfusion in the UK is considered safe, it is a complex process and mistakes can occur at various stages in the process.

In some scenarios transfusions carry an elevated risk of causing transfusion associated Graft versus Host Disease (TA-GvHD). This is a rare but usually lethal condition. At other times there may be a high risk of transfusion transmitted cytomegalovirus (CMV) infection.

To avoid these risks, patients with special requirements must be provided with irradiated and/or CMV negative blood components according to clinical guidelines.

Unfortunately, the prescription and administration of irradiated and cytomegalovirus (CMV) negative blood components continues to be an error-prone area of transfusion medicine.

Every year close to one hundred mistakes related to failures to meet these special requirements are reported to the UK haemovigilance scheme SHOT. Fortunately the likelyhood of developing TA-GvHD or CMV infection is small, but these mistakes continue to put patients at unnecessary risk.

This trial will increase knowledge and awareness of the problem, and attempt to gauge the effectiveness of interventions in reducing certain kinds of mistakes.
