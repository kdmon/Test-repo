This is the home page for the Scottish SPECIAL online clinical trial.

Stop and Prevent Errors in CMV-negative and Irradiated blood transfusions through App-based Learning.

Explore the sections below to find out more.

Objectives

  1. What is the current state of knowledge of the indications for irradiated and CMV-negative blood components among doctors and nurses who prescribe blood?

  2. How do access to a mobile app influence prescription behaviour for irradiated and CMV-negative blood in simulated patient scenarios?

  3. How do prescribers rate the mobile app and existing local and UK guidelines?

Background

Although blood transfusion in the UK is very safe, it is a complex process and mistakes can occur at various stages.

Some patients have an elevated risk of developing transfusion-associated Graft versus Host Disease (TA-GvHD). This is a very rare but usually lethal condition that can be avoided by irradiating cellular blood components.

Transfusion can also transmit cytomegalovirus (CMV) infection, which can lead to serious conditions in vulnerable patients. CMV-negative blood components should therefore be provided to these patients.

Every year approximately one hundred mistakes related to failures to provide irradiated and/or CMV negative blood components according to clinical guidelines are reported to the UK haemovigilance scheme SHOT.

This trial will investigate the effectiveness of an app-based learning intervention in reducing mistakes and raising awareness of the underlying causes.

Participate in the trial

The trial is currently active and recruiting. If you match the inclusion criteria, we would like to invite you to participate in the trial.

Inclusion criteria

  • Qualified nurse authoriser based on Scotland.
  • Doctor attached to the NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde healthboard.

Exclusion criteria

  • Nurses who do not have blood transfusion authoriser status.
  • Nurses outside of Scotland
  • Doctor attached healthboards other than NHS Greater Glasgow and Clyde.
