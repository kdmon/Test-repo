Welcome to SPECIAL

Stopping and Preventing Errors in CMV-negative and Irradiated blood transfusions through App-based Learning.

This is the home page for the Scottish SPECIAL online clinical trial. Explore the sections below to find out more.

Background

Although blood transfusion in the UK is very safe, it is a complex process and mistakes can occur at various stages.

Some patients have an elevated risk of developing transfusion-associated Graft versus Host Disease (TA-GvHD). This is a very rare but usually lethal condition that can be avoided by irradiating cellular blood components.

Transfusion can also transmit cytomegalovirus (CMV) infection, which can lead to serious conditions in vulnerable patients. CMV-negative blood components should therefore be provided to these patients.

Every year approximately one hundred mistakes related to failures to provide irradiated and CMV-negative blood components to patients according to guidelines are reported to the UK haemovigilance scheme SHOT.

This trial will investigate the effectiveness of an app-based learning intervention in enhancing prescribers' knowledge and raising awareness of the underlying causes.

Objectives

  1. What is the current state of knowledge of the indications for irradiated and CMV-negative blood components among doctors and nurses who prescribe blood?

  2. How do access to a mobile app influence prescription of irradiated and CMV-negative blood in simulated patient scenarios?

  3. How do prescribers rate the mobile app and existing interventions, such as local and UK guidelines?

Methodology

Randomised controlled trial comparing participants' prescribing behaviour under two conditions:

  1. Current practice based on access to local and national guidelines (control group).

  2. Novel intervention based on access to an app with learning material (treatment group)

Participants are randomly allocated one of the trial arms. To provide a baseline and capture group variance, participants are asked to answer a pre- and post-test with ten questions based on simulated patient scenarios.

Participate

The trial is currently active and recruiting.

If you match the inclusion criteria, please read the terms of participation and join the trial.

Inclusion criteria

Inclusion criteria:

  • Nurses and doctors authorised to prescribe blood transfusion and who are working in Scotland.

Exclusion criteria:

  • Nurses who do not have blood transfusion authoriser status.
  • Nurses and doctors working outside of Scotland.

Terms of participation

Please read this section carefully.

If you choose to participate, you will be asked to:

  1. Answer ten questions about irradiated and CMV-negative blood to the best of your knowledge without consulting any external source.
  2. Take some time to explore either local or national guideline, or a mobile app.
  3. Amend your answers based on any new information you may have gained.
  4. Evaluate the quality of the information resource.
  5. Finally, if you would like to participate in follow up survey, you will be asked to answer a brief questionnaire one month later.

Use of your data

  1. Any data you provide will be treated confidentially.
  2. Your answers will be made anonymous in any publications.
  3. You may withdraw from the research at any time for any reason.
  4. Data will not be shared outwith the research team and held securely.
  5. Personally identifiable information will not be collected unless you choose to disclose a contact email should you wish to also participate in the follow up survey.

Please contact us if you have any questions about any of the above. See contact details above.

By proceeding you are indicating your informed consent to participate in the research.
